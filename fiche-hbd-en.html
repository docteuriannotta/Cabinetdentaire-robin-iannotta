Oral Hygiene
A practical guide to healthy teeth and gums
BRUSHING
Frequency & duration
  • At least twice a day (morning + evening)
  • Average duration: 3 to 4 minutes
  • Wait 15–30 minutes after a meal

Manual technique
  • Hold brush at 45° to the gum line
  • Small, gentle circular motions
  • All surfaces: outer, inner, chewing
  • Do not brush the tongue (unless a smoker)
Electric brush technique
  • Brush tooth by tooth without harsh movements
  • Moisten the brush head before use
  • 3 seconds per tooth surface (ignore the timer)
  • Replace the brush head every 3 months

Toothpaste
  • Fluoride toothpaste (1450 ppm for adults)
  • Amount: pea-sized
  • Do not rinse thoroughly after brushing
Plaque control

Disclosing tablets or solution stain areas that have not been properly cleaned, making plaque visible to the naked eye.

  • After brushing, chew a tablet or apply a few drops of solution
  • Rinse lightly — coloured areas reveal residual plaque
  • Re-brush until the colour disappears
  • Use 1–2 times per week to improve your technique
  • Reveals neglected areas: inner surfaces, molars, gums
  • Available in pharmacies without prescription
Dentine hypersensitivity

Sensitivity to heat, cold or sugar when dentine is exposed: eroded enamel, gum recession, or overly vigorous brushing.

  • Brushing too hard or using a hard brush: erodes enamel and pushes back the gum
  • Repeated acidic drinks: progressively erode enamel
  • Avoid abrasive toothpastes (whitening, charcoal)
  • Use a remineralising toothpaste (stannous fluoride, hydroxyapatite or arginine): Elmex Sensitive, Sensodyne Repair & Protect or GUM Sensivital+
  • Massage onto sensitive areas without rinsing
  • Consult if it persists: may indicate a cavity or progressive recession
INTERDENTAL SPACES

Brushing alone cleans only 60% of tooth surfaces. The spaces between teeth require daily supplementary care.

Dental floss
  • Unwind ~30–40 cm, wrap around the middle fingers
  • Form a "C" shape around each tooth
  • Slide gently without forcing against the gum on each side
  • Remove floss toward the lips (do not slide back through the contact point)
Interdental brushes
  • Ideal when spaces are wider (from 0.6 mm)
  • Choose the right size for each space
  • Insert gently, with a few back-and-forth passes
  • Especially recommended after a bridge or implant
DIET & ORAL HEALTH
To limit
  • Sugar between meals
  • Fizzy drinks and fruit juice
  • Hard sweets and very sticky foods
To favour
  • Tap water (fluoridated)
  • A healthy and varied diet
  • Xylitol gum after meals
ORAL IRRIGATOR (WATER FLOSSER)

An oral irrigator projects a fine pulsed water stream to dislodge food debris and plaque from hard-to-reach spaces. It is an excellent supplement to brushing and flossing, not a replacement.

Benefits
  • Highly effective around implants, bridges and orthodontic appliances
  • Stimulates and massages the gums
  • Reduces gingival bleeding within a few weeks of regular use
  • Easy to use for those with reduced dexterity
How to use it
  • Fill the reservoir with water
  • Aim the tip toward the interdental space, slightly angled toward the gum
  • Slowly run along each space, lips slightly closed
  • Use daily, ideally after evening brushing
  • Keep pressure low to avoid injuring the tissue
KEYES PASTE
⚠ Reserved for patients with a diagnosed periodontal disease

Keyes paste must never be used as self-medication or without the prior approval of your dental surgeon or periodontist. Its use is exclusively within the scope of a prescribed and supervised periodontal treatment.

Keyes paste is an antiseptic mixture used as a supplement to the mechanical treatment of periodontal diseases (gingivitis and periodontitis). It traditionally combines pharmaceutical-grade sodium bicarbonate and hydrogen peroxide, sometimes with a fluoride toothpaste. Its mechanical and antibacterial action targets the anaerobic bacteria responsible for destroying periodontal tissue.

How to apply (on prescription)
  • Apply the paste onto a soft toothbrush
  • Gently massage the gingival sulcus and interdental spaces
  • Leave for 1–2 minutes, then spit without vigorous rinsing
  • Frequency and duration as defined by your practitioner
Points of caution
  • Never substitute Keyes paste for professional periodontal treatment (scaling, root planing)
  • Prolonged unsupervised use may abrade enamel
  • Report any discomfort or worsening to your practitioner
  • Ineffective — even counterproductive — without confirmed periodontal disease
FOLLOW-UP & DENTAL VISITS
Recommended frequency

1 visit every 6 to 12 months, even with no pain.


Scaling

Removes tartar that brushing alone cannot eliminate.
X-rays

Detect invisible cavities and monitor the alveolar bone.


Signs to watch for

Bleeding, sensitivity, persistent pain: consult without delay.
Good daily oral hygiene is the first dental treatment — and the least costly.

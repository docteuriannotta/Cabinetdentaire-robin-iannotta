External Teeth Whitening
Patient guide to a brighter, healthier smile
WHAT IS EXTERNAL TEETH WHITENING?

Teeth whitening is a cosmetic treatment that reduces discolouration of the enamel and dentine. It uses gels based on hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, which diffuse through the enamel to break down pigmented molecules.

Discolourations that can be treated
  • Yellowing caused by age and time
  • Stains from coffee, tea, red wine, tobacco
  • Discolouration related to root canal treatment and/or previous trauma (specific protocol)
What whitening cannot do
  • Whiten crowns, veneers or composite fillings
  • Achieve a shade beyond what nature allows
  • Replace a professional scale and polish
THE GOLD STANDARD: CUSTOM-MADE TRAYS

The current reference technique uses thermoformed trays made from an impression of your teeth, combined with a gel provided by your practitioner.

Treatment process
  • Physical or digital impression of your arches
  • Custom tray fabrication in the laboratory
  • Carbamide peroxide gel (10–16%)
  • Wear the tray overnight for 3 to 6 weeks
  • Intermediate check-up recommended by your practitioner
Advantages of this method
  • Progressive, even results across all teeth
  • Gel concentration adjusted to your sensitivity level
  • Reusable trays for future touch-ups
  • Treatment carried out at home, at your own pace
  • Currently recommended standard for external whitening
ARE YOU A GOOD CANDIDATE?
Favourable indications
  • Healthy natural teeth, without untreated cavities
  • Healthy gums (no active periodontitis)
  • Teeth stained by tobacco, coffee or age
  • Realistic expectations about the final result
Contraindications
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women
  • Children and adolescents under 18
  • Severe untreated dentine hypersensitivity
  • Significant enamel erosion
  • Allergy to peroxides
POSSIBLE SIDE EFFECTS
⚠ Tooth sensitivity — normal and temporary

Hypersensitivity to cold or air may occur during treatment. It generally disappears within 24–72 hours after the end of treatment. If it persists, inform your practitioner so the protocol can be adjusted.

Other possible effects
  • Mild gum irritation if the gel overflows
  • Temporary sensation of "numb" teeth
  • Uneven whitening (more porous areas)
How to minimise them
  • Use a sensitive teeth toothpaste before treatment
  • Follow the recommended application times
  • Avoid very hot or cold foods for 48 hours
AFTER TREATMENT — ADVICE AND DURATION

The first 48 hours are critical: teeth are more porous and absorb pigments easily. Follow the "white diet" (foods and drinks low in colour during the first 48 hours of treatment).

To avoid (throughout treatment)
  • Coffee, tea, red wine, coloured fruit juices
  • Tomato sauce, beetroot, curry, soy sauce
  • Tobacco and coloured lipstick
To maintain the result
  • Brush twice a day with fluoride toothpaste
  • Rinse after coffee or wine
  • Use a straw for tannin-rich drinks
Duration of results

On average 1 to 3 years depending on lifestyle. Results last longer in non-smokers.
Cost and reimbursement

A cosmetic procedure, not reimbursed by French Social Security. Price varies depending on the impression method (physical or digital).
WARNING — UNREGULATED TREATMENTS
🚫 "Smile bars" and social media products: real risks

These practices are illegal in France and dangerous. Only dental surgeons are authorised to perform or prescribe teeth whitening. The use of concentrated gels by non-professionals has been prohibited since 2012 (European Directive 2011/84/EU).

Health risks
  • Severe chemical burns to the gums and oral mucosa
  • Irreversible enamel damage (demineralisation)
  • Chronic dentine hypersensitivity and persistent pain
  • Undosed, counterfeit or uncontrolled products
  • No prior assessment: cavities and lesions go undetected
Warning signs
  • Very low prices, treatments in beauty salons or institutes
  • Kits sold on Instagram, TikTok or uncertified websites
  • Spectacular before/after results in just minutes
  • No prior dental consultation or examination
  • Products without French labelling or CE marking
FOLLOW-UP & FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Recommended follow-up

Frequency

A touch-up session every 12 to 18 months is enough to maintain the result, depending on your hygiene and habits.

Signs to watch for

If sensitivity persists for more than 5 days or if you notice prolonged gum irritation, consult without delay.
Frequently asked questions

Is it painful?

No, the treatment is painless. Mild sensitivity is possible but temporary.

Are the results guaranteed?

Results depend on the nature of the discolouration and the structure of your enamel. A prior assessment allows the expected result to be estimated.
